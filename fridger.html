BACKGROUND

Context

In Spring 2021, I took a class Application Lab - an intensive project-driven course that helps students to explore and practice design, prototyping, and innovation. For this class, I was challenged to design 3 application prorotypes in 3 weeks . The challenge prompt for the week 2 was - to develop an application that helps another person solve a tiny problem.

Process

RESEARCH

Inspiration

Living in a residential college with a convenience of dining hall, I don't have to worry about cooking. During a casual conversation with a friend who cooks at home, I found out that cooking routine can be tiring and stressful. Therefore, I decided to solve a problem for people who cook at home.

User Interview

To design an application that solves the real problem that people face, I interviewed potential clients - people who cook at home. Here are some of the useful takeaway from the user-interview.
